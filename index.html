Transitional Housing

What is Transitional Housing?

  • Provides temporary housing
  • Provides programs to develop skills to help members stay housed
  • Addresses physical and mental conditions
  • Provides case workers and counselors to support members
  • Quote from

Why is Transitional Housing?

  • Research has shown transitional housing to be more successful than its emergency shelter counterparts
  • Transitional housing helps build the skills of individuals who would otherwise have no way to help themselves
  • Transitional housing provides crucial drug rehabilitation resources that may not be available otherwise
  • Staff provide oversite to the individuals to ensure they are progressing towards finding a permanent living situation

Evaluation of Transitional housing programs from individuals in the programs

  • Renee
    • The life skills classes didn’t only help me, they helped me help others in the program. When I needed assistance writing my resume, the staff people helped me write it up and I put it into a format, using examples they gave me to use. So I saw I could do this, it wasn’t as bad as I thought it would be. Then I started helping the other people who couldn’t quite get their ideas put on paper or their few jobs in order. That made me feel good to have learned something and show someone else how to do it.
  • Charita, A single Mother
    • I have always been a hard worker, and have never minded working. But, it has always been hard for me to get a job simply because I’ve only worked in cleaning jobs. I do have a high school diploma but I didn’t have much job experience to offer or to put on a job application. But when I went to Estival Place, in the job training classes the instructor helped me get enrolled at State Tech, where I took some clerical courses. I can type and do some filing now. That helped me get my job I got now working at an office as a clerk.
  • Aaron
    • I had no job and nowhere to go. I had lived in three different shelters before living at Estival. I had my baby boy and no place to stay. When I moved in Estival, the case manager there helped me get the job I am still working on now. I’ve been here about 2 years. Oh, when they told me about the job, I went to fill out an application at the agency’s office. The people at the agency were very nice and happy … seemed so nice and were glad that I was from Estival place. So after I filled out the application, they called me for an interview. Then I got the job and have been here since.
  • Shaena
    • When I was in my11th month at Estival, my case manager referred me to a bunch of places to apply for permanent housing. She told about the rent, how the people kept up the outside of them, how they had a low up-front deposit rate. She helped me a lot … she even helped get the place I’m in now!
